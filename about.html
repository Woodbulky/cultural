A Civilization of Timeless Wisdom

Over 5,000 Years of Cultural Excellence

India, the birthplace of some of humanity's oldest civilizations, has been a beacon of culture, spirituality, and intellectual achievement for millennia. The Indus Valley Civilization, one of the world's earliest urban cultures, laid the foundation for the rich tapestry of Indian heritage.

From the philosophical depths of the Vedas and Upanishads to the mathematical brilliance that gave the world the concept of zero, India has contributed immeasurably to human knowledge and culture.

Unity in Diversity

India's greatest strength lies in its harmonious blend of diverse cultures, languages, and traditions.

22 Languages

Officially recognized languages, with over 19,500 dialects spoken across the nation.

All Faiths

Birthplace of Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism, welcoming all religions.

28 States

Each state with unique customs, cuisine, attire, and cultural expressions.

Diverse Arts

Classical and folk arts spanning music, dance, painting, and craftsmanship.

Core Cultural Values

Principles That Guide Indian Society

Atithi Devo Bhava

"Guest is God" - The ancient tradition of hospitality that treats every guest with divine respect.

Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam

"The World is One Family" - A philosophy of universal brotherhood and global harmony.

Ahimsa

Non-violence - A principle championed by Mahatma Gandhi that changed the world.

Dharma

Righteous duty - Living according to moral and ethical principles.

Journey Through Time

Key milestones in India's civilizational journey.

3300-1300 BCE

Indus Valley Civilization

One of the world's earliest urban civilizations with advanced city planning, drainage systems, and trade networks.

1500-500 BCE

Vedic Period

Composition of the Vedas, Upanishads, and the foundation of Hindu philosophy and Sanskrit literature.

322-185 BCE

Mauryan Empire

Emperor Ashoka spread Buddhism and established one of the largest empires in ancient India.

1526-1857

Mughal Era

A golden age of art, architecture, and culture that gave us the Taj Mahal and Indo-Islamic heritage.

Common Questions

Learn more about India's cultural heritage

Namaste represents the belief that there is a Divine spark within each of us that is located in the heart chakra. The gesture is an acknowledgment of the soul in one by the soul in another.

The Indian Constitution recognizes 22 official languages. However, there are hundreds of other languages and thousands of dialects spoken across the country, making India one of the most linguistically diverse nations.

The Golden Triangle is a tourist circuit which connects the national capital Delhi, Agra (home of the Taj Mahal), and Jaipur (the Pink City). It is called "golden" for the extraordinary wealth of cultural and historical splendor on offer in each of the three cities.
