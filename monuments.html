UNESCO World Heritage Sites

India has 40+ UNESCO World Heritage Sites showcasing its rich architectural heritage.

UNESCO

Taj Mahal

Agra, Uttar Pradesh

Symbol of eternal love, this ivory-white marble mausoleum is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture.

UNESCO

Red Fort

Delhi

The iconic symbol of India's independence, this Mughal fortress is a testament to royal grandeur.

UNESCO

Qutub Minar

Delhi

A 73-meter tall victory tower, the world's tallest brick minaret with intricate carvings.

Palace

Hawa Mahal

Jaipur, Rajasthan

The iconic "Palace of Winds" with 953 small windows designed for royal women.

Monument

Gateway of India

Mumbai

An iconic arch-monument overlooking the Arabian Sea, built to commemorate King George V's visit.

Temple

Meenakshi Temple

Madurai, Tamil Nadu

A historic Hindu temple with 14 colorful gopurams and thousands of sculptures.

UNESCO

Hampi

Karnataka

Ancient village dotted with numerous ruined temple complexes from the Vijayanagara Empire.

UNESCO

Ajanta & Ellora

Maharashtra

Masterpieces of Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain rock-cut religious art and architecture.

UNESCO

Konark Sun Temple

Odisha

13th-century Sun Temple built in the shape of a gigantic chariot with stone wheels.
